How it unfolded

Twelve days that changed everything

I went from protecting a running streak to protecting a surgical repair. Open any milestone for the fuller story.

014 Jul 2026

The first fall

A road reflector caught my foot during an 8 km run.

Read more +

I fell forward, over-strided and felt something pop high in my left hamstring. I finished the run and kept running over the following days, although slowly and with increasing difficulty.

029 Jul 2026

The second fall

One slip turned a painful injury into something much bigger.

Read more +

My heel shot forward on the injured side. The pain and loss of function increased substantially. This became my last unbroken run and the end of a 4,435-day running streak.

0313 Jul 2026

Physio assessment

Calvin recognised that this needed urgent imaging.

Read more +

The weakness, bruising and inability to use the hamstring properly raised concern for a significant tear. An MRI was arranged for the following day.

0414 Jul 2026

MRI

The scan revealed a complete proximal hamstring avulsion.

Read more +

All three hamstring tendon attachments had pulled away from the bone. The physio called the next day and arranged an urgent sports orthopaedic consultation.

0515 Jul 2026

Surgeon

The verdict was immediate: “We’re fixing this tomorrow.”

Read more +

The surgeon reviewed the MRI after work on Wednesday night. With all three tendon attachments involved, the repair could not wait.

0616 Jul 2026

Surgery

The hamstring tendons were reattached to the bone.

Read more +

Surgery repaired the complete avulsion. Because this involved all three tendon attachments, the initial protection phase is stricter than it would be for a smaller tear.

0717 Jul 2026

Home

The six-week, zero-weight-bearing phase begins.

I made the journey home on crutches. For six weeks there can be no weight through the repaired leg while the tendon-to-bone healing gets underway.

These phases are a guide only. Every progression depends on the surgeon, the physio and how the repair responds.

Zero weight through the left leg, crutches for every transfer, pain and swelling management, and only surgeon-approved movement.

02Next

Weeks 6–12

Controlled loading

Subject to surgical review, gradually reintroduce weight bearing, movement and early strengthening under physio guidance.

Progress strength, balance and everyday function. Milestones will be based on capacity, not just the calendar.

A careful, criteria-led return. No promised date, no shortcuts and no risking the repair for the sake of the clock.

Latest update · 18 July 2026

Home, repaired and learning the crutch life

Surgery is done and all three hamstring tendon attachments are back on the bone. I’m now home with the strict instruction that no weight goes through the left leg for six weeks.

The immediate challenge is less athletic than logistical: moving safely, carrying the basics, managing discomfort and resisting the urge to do too much. For someone used to running at least eight kilometres every day, stillness is a completely different kind of discipline.
